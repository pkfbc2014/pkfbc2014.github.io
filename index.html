Excellent Student

Here are some of the best performing students in our project, let’s hear what they have to say.

  • During my three months of teaching in rural Kenya, I deeply realized that education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty. This experience taught me to solve problems from the perspective of local people and also strengthened my belief in dedicating myself to the development of international education.

    John McIntyre

    Undergraduate, Columbia University

  • The medical aid project in the slums of Kolkata completely reshaped my professional perception. A mother once insisted on using the saved milk tea money to pay for medical expenses, and this sense of dignity made me understand that medical assistance is not charity, but a joint construction of equal rights. In the future, I will devote myself to the global healthcare industry with this consciousness.

    John McIntyre

    Undergraduate, University of Oxford

  • In an ecological project at an altitude of 3800 meters in the Andes Mountains, we collaborated with the indigenous people of Quechua to build a sustainable toilet system. This journey taught me that true sustainable development must be rooted in traditional cultural wisdom, and modern technology should be a service provider rather than a transformer.

    John McIntyre

    Postgraduate, Stanford University

  • Participating in the 180 day post disaster reconstruction of Dongliza Lake is a journey of growth intertwined with sweat and hope. This experience made me realize that charity is not a one-sided rescue, but a mutual healing between wounded souls. In the future, I will continue to strive for building resilient communities for vulnerable groups.

    John McIntyre

    Postgraduate, MIT
